Pull ups

How to do it:
1. Hang from the bar with your hands slightly wider than your shoulders in an overhand grip.
2. Depress your lats,than pull up until your chin comes slightly over the bar.
3. control the descent back down until your arms are fully extended back in the starting position.

3-4 sets of 8-12 reps

Chin ups

How to do it:
1. Hang onto a pull-up bar with an underhand grip placed at shoulder width.
2. bend your knees and cross your lower legs, engaging your core as you hand.
3. Pull your body up until your chin is level with your hands on the bar.
4. Pause as your biceps experiences maximum tension before lowering back down to the start position.

3-4 sets of 8-12 reps

Barbell row

How to do it:
1. Stand upright with your feet under your hips, holding a barbell with a double-overhand or double-underhand grip.
2. Inhale and perform a hip hinge, tipping over and allowing the bar to glide down until it hangs just below your kneecap.
3. From here, brace your core; your torso should be parallel-ish to the floor.
4. Hold your air and row the barbell up toward your lower abdomen.

3-4 sets of 8-12 reps

Close grip pull-down

How to do it:
1. Adjust the weight of a lat pulldown station until it is moderately heavy and then fix a close-grip handle to the carabiner.
2. Sit down into the lat pulldown station, gripping the handles with each hand and firmly fixing your thighs against the pad.
3. Lean back very slightly and pull the cable downward, driving your elbows into your back pockets and squeezing your shoulders together at the bottom.

3-4 sets of 8-12 reps

Seated cable row

How to do it:
1. Take a seat on the row station and grab the handles with a close, neutral grip.
2. Scoot back on the bench to pull the cable taut. Allow the weight to pull your shoulders forward while you sit upright with a braced core.
3. Row the handle (or handles) toward your body, pulling your elbows down and backward.

3-4 sets of 8-12 reps

Dumb-bell/Bar-bell pullover

How to do it:
1. Grab a dumbbell/barbell and sit on the end of a weight bench.
2. Sit back and lie down on the bench. Hold the insides of one end of the dumbbell/barbell in your palms with your arms extended directly upward, elbows straight.
3. From here, gradually lower the dumbbell back behind your head without bending your elbows.
4. Once your arms are parallel to your torso (if your shoulder mobility permits), reverse the motion and contract your lats to bring the dumbbell/barbell back to the starting position.

3-4 sets of 8-12 reps

Barbell row

How to do it:
1. Lay a moderate to heavy dumbbell beside a weight bench and place the knee opposite your working arm onto the bench. Plant your non-working palm on the bench as well.
2. Firmly “spike” your back foot into the floor behind you or out to the side. Brace your core.
3. Reach down with your working arm and grab the dumbbell, then take a deep belly breath.
4. Row the dumbbell up until your upper arm is parallel with your torso, then lower it slowly back down without letting it touch the floor again.

3-4 sets of 8-12 reps
